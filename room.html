Davis Library

Davis Library Description

  • Davis Library is UNC-Chapel Hills main research library, located near The Pit and opened in 1984.
  • Named after Walter Royal Davis, it houses over 3.5 million volumes in the humanities, social sciences, business, and foreign languages.
  • The eight-story building offers quiet study areas, group study rooms, the Music Library, and the Epigraphy Room.
  • Equipped with public computers, instructional labs, and extensive digital resources.
  • Accessibility services include assistive technologies and book retrieval.
  • Open late most days, Davis Library is a vital hub for study, research, and collaboration at UNC.
  • Photo by University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill on https://library.unc.edu/locations/davis-library/
Undergraduate Library

Undergraduate Library Description

  • The R.B. House Undergraduate Library at UNC-Chapel Hill opened in 1968 to support undergraduate learning.
  • Located near The Pit and Wilson Library, it was the first on campus to allow direct access to the stacks.
  • It features a focused book collection, popular reading materials, and a large media collection.
  • The library provides group study rooms, quiet study spaces, public computers, and digital resources.
  • Accessibility services include assistive technologies and accessible entrances.
  • Open most days from 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM, it’s a key space for study, research, and collaboration for undergraduates.
  • Photo by University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill on https://library.unc.edu/news/undergraduate-library-will-offer-overnight-study-hours-during-exams/
Wilson Library

Wilson Library Description

  • Wilson Library is UNC-Chapel Hill’s main special collections library, located on Polk Place.
  • Opened in 1929 and named after librarian Louis Round Wilson.
  • Originally served as the university’s main library before Davis Library was built.
  • Houses five major research collections.
  • Features the grand Fearrington Reading Room for research and study.
  • Hosts exhibitions, lectures, and public programs throughout the year.
  • Open to students, scholars, and the public for rare and archival research.
  • Photo by University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill on https://library.unc.edu/locations/wilson-special-collections-library/
